## **Featured community content**

Here are some great apps and tutorials by the Streamlit community. Check out the [Streamlit forum](https://streamlit.ghost.io/p/efb2b033-a8e4-47ec-bb60-f1313d84052a/discuss.streamlit.io) for even more community content.

* Daniel's [Carbon Intensity App](https://twitter.com/sciDelta/status/1480175342828589059?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) lets you visualize the carbon intensity for the UK by date and can forecast up to 2 days ahead.
* In his video [Pseudo Grid Layout using Streamlit columns](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=clFrWjiwxL0&ref=streamlit.ghost.io), Fanilo shows how to use horizontal layout and columns to improvise a grid layout.
* Say or write a pickup line, and Paul's [Pickup Line Prediction Using K-Nearest Neighbors](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/paul-fentress-985ab7112_sidemen-tinder-pickup-line-prediction-using-activity-6884064491286466560-Eodk?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) app will determine if it's a success or a failure.
* Simulate and study emergent properties of teamwork with Michael and Chris's [SuperScript](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/michaelchristen_agentbasedmodel-futureofwork-datadriven-activity-6887737348184952832-Nzvu?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) app.
* Learn all about these [5 Streamlit Components To Build Better Applications](https://towardsdatascience.com/5-streamlit-components-to-build-better-applications-71e0195c82d4?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) in Ahmed's breakdown.
* Sebastian's app teaches about the [Confusion Matrix](https://twitter.com/sebastiandres/status/1481774731800068096?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) in an interactive and fun way.
* Learn how to build a [Real-Time Live Finance/Marketing/Data Science Dashboard in Python](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OkodDZxsN1I&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) from 1littlecoder's YouTube tutorial.
* Sejal does [A Deep Dive into Wordle, the New Pandemic Puzzle Craze](https://towardsdatascience.com/a-deep-dive-into-wordle-the-new-pandemic-puzzle-craze-9732d97bf723?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) with an app to analyze how effective your guesses are.
* Siavash created [Koffee of the world](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/siavash-yasini_coffee-data-visualization-activity-6891179821968293888-GdTv?ref=streamlit.ghost.io)‚Äîa simple web app to explore, visualize, and compare coffee quality profiles of different countries.
* Find Google Earth Engine apps by their location, filter by zoom level, and open the app directly in your browser with Philipp's [Earth Engine App Finder](https://twitter.com/Mixed_Pixels/status/1486058156853760001?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io).
* Use Antoine's [Download data from archive.org](https://twitter.com/antoineripret/status/1486653886328938499?s=20&t=SwRs6pYHMXP6WEfhGxXQjQ&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) app to get old URLs for a domain‚Äîa necessary step for fixing migrations.
