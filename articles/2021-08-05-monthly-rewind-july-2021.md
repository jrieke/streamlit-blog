## **Featured community content**

Some great apps, videos, and articles by the Streamlit community. Check out the [Streamlit forum](https://streamlit.ghost.io/p/efb2b033-a8e4-47ec-bb60-f1313d84052a/discuss.streamlit.io) for even more community content.

* Dr. WJB Mattingly kicks off his Streamlit tutorial series with a video on [How to use Streamlit in 30 Minutes!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=43RJ3JByygE&ref=streamlit.ghost.io)
* Upload an image to Ujjayanta's app and it will [Generate ASCII images using GAN](https://twitter.com/I_m_Jojo/status/1412091610603552773?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io)
* This [Cloud Optimized GeoTIFF Viewer](https://twitter.com/MykolaKozyr/status/1412467043023085568?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) by Mykola lets you view your COG files with an interactive web map
* Anmol's [Nightingale Chart Plotter](https://twitter.com/slothfulwave612/status/1416459775563292674?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) app lets you create, customize and download Nightingale charts with mplsoccer
* Matthew and his colleagues created a dashboard to [Visualize your EED data](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/matthew-pin_datascience-datavisualization-electrophysiology-activity-6820481867054157824-M4LX?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) which can generate six unique visualizations as seen from a variety of different perspectives
* The Python Engineer gives a speedy tutorial in his [Machine Learning Web App In Python In 60 Seconds! #Shorts](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yhMwWPNP9VM&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) video
* Gain insight into, measure the quality of, and repair your data with the [DataQtor](https://twitter.com/baligoyem/status/1417858437673803780?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) app by Beytullah
* Lukas' [Animation des Rheinabflusses seit Juni 2020](https://twitter.com/calmbach/status/1419547086538821640?s=20&ref=streamlit.ghost.io) consecutively displays the precipitation and runoff data of the Rhine in Basel
* Yuichiro forked Kazuhito's [Tokyo2020 Pictogram using MediaPipe](https://twitter.com/whitphx_ja/status/1420442166740996098?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) code and made a Streamlit app so anyone can try it!
* Cassie developed a [Dungeons & Dragons Currency Converter](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cassie-nutter_streamlit-python-games-activity-6826181230095085568-4Ss9?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) to calculate which and how many coins to use when spending in the game
* Check out Kieran's [simple web publishing system](https://discuss.streamlit.io/t/simple-web-publishing-system/15635?ref=streamlit.ghost.io) which can display both text based and interactive code-based posts
